    • Research Experiences

  • Accuracy Evaluation and Error Correction of Extreme Precipitation Based on GPM IMERG in Poyang Lake Basin

    • The Integrated Multi-satellitE Retrievals for GPM (IMERG) algorithm combines information from the GPM satellite constellation to estimate precipitation over the majority of the Earth's surface.Although there are studies on the precision evaluation of GPM satellite precipitation products, there are few studies on Poyang Lake basin and few studies on extreme precipitation, so the applicability of GPM in extreme rainfall in Poyang Lake basin is further studied.

    GPM IMERG remote sensing imagery is for reference only

    Explore what factors affect the observation of precipitation by GPM satellites and what effects these factors have on remote sensing satellite observations. Start with microscopic geomorphological factors: elevation, aspect, and slope, and further analyze macroscopic factors such as geomorphology, soil cover, and more. In addition to this, an in-depth cross-analysis is carried out from a time perspective. The evaluation indicators are : Relative BIAS (RB) , Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) and Pearson correlation coefficient (CC).

    According to the experiment, GPM IMERG precipitation is not very ideal under extreme precipitation conditions, and needs to be further optimized.

    The Bayesian neural network is applied to correct the GPM IMERG precipitation under the extreme precipitation situation in Jiangxi Province. One input layer, 15 hidden layers and one output layer are set. The input variables were: GPM precipitation data, slope, slope direction, longitude, latitude, altitude and NDVI data. The expected output is the measured precipitation on the ground, and the actual output is the corrected GPM precipitation.

  • Extraction of Silt Dams in Loess Small Watersheds Based on Multi-source Data

    • The precise location of silt dams is of great importance to the study of watershed soils. Now we combine remote sensing technology and geoinformation technology, and introduce deep learning (U-Net network) to obtain the silt area of the Jiuyuangou watershed.     I would like to say, this new method is very cool! which has significantly improved accuracy verification results compared with the traditional object-oriented classification extraction method, with 81% accuracy and 89% completeness of silt dam extraction.

  • GIS-based Interactive Map of Campus Cryers' Distribution

    • Psychogeography describes the effect of a geographical location on the emotions and behaviour of individuals.

    Based on this psychological effect, a "GIS based interactive map of cryers' distribution" was created. Through the anonymous questionnaire survey mode, everyone's stories are collected, and each story creates a point element, recording the spatial location of the weiler to vent their emotions, and the story attributes. According to gestalt psychology, black and blue underlays will be selected, with point elements expressed in light blue tear-like symbols.

    Publish the map to ArcGIS Online to share these stories, especially those friends who are anxious and depressed, and make them feel not lonely. According to the empathy effect, can stand in the other party's point of view, through leaving messages on the website, give encouragement and help.
